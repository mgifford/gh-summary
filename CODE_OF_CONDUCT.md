# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The maintainers of `mgifford/gh-summary` are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, and inclusive community.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We value diverse perspectives and constructive collaboration. Participation in this project is governed by mutual respect, professionalism, and consideration for others.

## Standards for Behavior

### Be Respectful

Treat all community members with respect. Engage constructively with ideas. Assume good intent, but take responsibility for impact. Disagreements are expected. Disrespect is not.

Examples of respectful behavior include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Listening to and acknowledging different viewpoints
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback gracefully
- Showing empathy toward other community members

### Be Professional

Act in a manner consistent with a professional open source community. Keep discussions focused on the project. Address issues directly and factually.

Examples of professional behavior include:

- Focusing on technical merit and documented evidence
- Avoiding inflammatory or accusatory language
- Respecting project processes and decisions
- Following documented contribution guidelines

### Be Considerate

Consider how your actions affect others. Not everyone has the same background, context, or availability.

Examples of considerate behavior include:

- Being mindful of tone in written communication
- Allowing reasonable time for responses
- Providing clear, reproducible information in issues and pull requests
- Respecting boundaries and personal privacy

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in any project spaces:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Derogatory comments related to personal characteristics
- Personal attacks, insults, or trolling
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Threats of violence or encouraging harmful behavior

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- Reviewing and moderating issues, discussions, and pull requests
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to violations
- Documenting enforcement decisions when appropriate
- Protecting the privacy and safety of reporters

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Documentation
- Project-related communication channels

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples include:

- Using an official project email address
- Posting via official social media accounts
- Speaking at events as a project representative

### Enforcement Process

1. The maintainers will review the report.
2. Additional information may be requested if necessary.
3. A determination will be made based on the available evidence.
4. Appropriate corrective action will be taken.

### Consequences

Consequences may include:

- A private warning
- A public warning
- Temporary suspension from the project
- Permanent removal from project spaces

Maintainers may escalate consequences based on the severity or pattern of behavior.
